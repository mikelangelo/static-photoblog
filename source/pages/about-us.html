About Mikelangelo Photography

So… who is Michael Heliker?

Who I Am (the short version)

I started photography in the 1900s — back when film, darkrooms, and patience were required. What began as a way to support my fine art studies quickly turned into an obsession with one thing: capturing moments that only exist once.

Today, I photograph motorsports, equestrian events, and real-world action with the same goal I had back then — to preserve effort, emotion, and motion exactly as they felt in the moment.

What I Focus On

I look for the moments most people miss:

  • The breath before judge's whistle blows
  • The focus before the green flag drops
  • The split second of exhaustion, pride, or relief

I photograph people (and horses) doing hard things on purpose — and I do it in a way that feels honest, strong, and real.

My Approach
(calm, prepared, and out of your way)

Fast-paced environments are where I do my best work. I show up prepared, stay calm when things get chaotic, and never add stress to an already intense day.

I know when to step in, when to step back, and when to disappear completely. Most of the time, you won’t even notice me working — until you see the photos and realize I was standing in the perfect place at the perfect time.

How I Serve My Clients

I believe photography is a service, not just a product.

You can expect:

  • Clear communication and zero ego
  • Positive energy, even on long days
  • Fast turnaround and consistent results
  • A photographer who cares about your outcome as much as you do
  • Images that feel like the moment — not a reenactment of it

Working with me

I can be reached on Facebook or via mike@mikelangelo.com

A Bit More Human

When I’m not shooting, I’m teaching, building creative things with my kids, racing RC cars, or covered in dirt at a track with a camera in my hand. I’ve spent my career blending art and technology, which explains why I care equally about emotion and execution.

And yes — I still get excited every time I load photos into Lightroom.

Why This Matters

Long after the dust settles and the ribbons fade, photographs are what remain. They’re proof of effort, progress, and moments worth remembering.

If you’re doing something that matters to you, I’d love to help you hold onto it.

Let’s make something worth remembering.
